Jaisalmer

The Golden City

A city in the Indian state of Rajasthan known as "The Golden City," Jaisalmer is situated 575 kilometres (357 miles) west of the state's capital, Jaipur. The historic Jaisalmer Fort sits atop the town's ridge of yellowish sandstone. A royal palace and three elaborate Jain temples may be seen in this fort. Sandstone with intricate carvings is used to construct many of the homes and temples in the fort and the hamlet below. The town, which is located in the Great Indian Desert's Thar Desert, has a population of roughly 78,000 people overall, including those who live in the fort. The Jaisalmer District's administrative centre is there. The former capital of Jaisalmer State was Jaisalmer.

In the year 1156 AD, Rawal Jaisal established Jaisalmer. Jaisalmer refers to Jaisal's Hill Fort. Because of the use of yellow sandstone in the construction of the fort and the town below, which gives them both a distinct golden-yellow light, Jaisalmer is frequently referred to as the "Golden City of India."

HISTORY

In the 12th century, Rawal Jaisal the eldest son of the Rawal of Deoraj was passed over in favour of a younger half-brother for the throne of Laudrava.

Rawal Jaisal met a sage named Eesul who was staying on the rock while inspecting Trikuta, a big triangle rock rising more than 75 metres out of the surrounding dunes as a more safe place for a new capital. When he discovered that Jaisal was descended from the Yadhuvanshi clan, Eesul informed him that in accordance with mythology, Krishna and Bhima had come to this location for a ceremony, where Krishna had prophesied that a descendant of the Yadhuvanshi clan would someday establish a kingdom here. Eesul then showed him a spring that Krishna had created and his prophecy carved into a rock. This encounter gave Rawal encouragement, and despite Eesul's prediction that it would be attacked again, Rawal chose to relocate his capital to this area.

In 1156, Rawal Jaisal established his new capital in the form of a mud fort and named it Jaisalmer after himself. According to most historians, the Sikh Jats of Majha and Malwa Doab's and the rulers of Kapurthala state in Doaba in Punjab trace their direct lineage to Jaisalmer royal families over the centuries.

TOURISM

Jaisalmer is home to some of the most sought after attractions of the nation like the majestic Jaisalmer Fort, full of live desert national park, ancient Kuldhara Village, beautifully designed Jain temples, surreal Gadisar Lake, rustic Sam Sand Dunes, artistic Tazia Tower and Badal Palace, intricately designed Patwon ki Haveli, glorious bada bagh, and many more significant places of tourist attractions.
