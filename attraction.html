ATTRACTION of RAJASTHAN

The attractions of Rajasthan are many, occupying a valuable position on the map of Rajasthan. In truth, Rajasthan is a tourist paradise full of magnificent tourist attractions. The land has a lot to offer tourists, including opulent palaces, stunning havelis, magnificent forts, beautiful temples, captivating monuments, unique museums, luxurious train, romantic lakes, fascinating desert, appealing wildlife, breathtaking adventure, colourful fairs and festivals, delectable cuisine, wonderful handicrafts, and much more.Some of the most famous attractions of Rajasthan are Lake Palace - Udaipur, Hawa Mahal, City Palace, Umaidbhavan Palace, Ranthambore wildlife sanctuary, Dilwara temples, Havelis, Sam Sand Dunes and Palace On Wheels.

FLORA and FAUNA

Rajasthan is a land of Colors. While the Thar Desert covers one portion of it, the other is known for its lush greenery. The woods dominate the Aravallies and Vindhya mountains, accounting for around 9% of the total area. The most common tree in Rajasthan is kejri, also known as prosopis cineraria. Its bean-shaped fruit, sangri, is eaten. Other trees found here include thor, botdi babul, anwal, sewan, dhaman, boor, and bhatut.Rajasthan has a diverse biodiversity that may be divided into mammals, reptiles, and birds. Rajasthan is well-known for its tiger and endangered species habitats. Rajasthan is home to a variety of wildlife, including the nilgai, bluebull, chinkara, tiger, leopard, elephant, langur, jackal, wolf, sambar, chital, Indian porcupine, desert fox, Indian gerbil, five-striped palm squirrel, wild boar, endangered black buck, and many more.

Many wildlife sanctuaries are available in Rajasthan, including Keoladeo Ghana National Park, Sariska National Park, Desert National Park, Gajner Wildlife Sanctuary, and Ranthambore National Park. The woodlands of Rajasthan are home to migrating birds as well as stunning and colourful birds. The imperial sand grouse, falcons, buzzards, coots, ducks, pelicans, and uncommon Siberian cranes are among the avian species that may be found here.

WILDLIFE RESORTS in RAJASTHAN

Wildlife enthusiasts go from all over the world to Rajasthan to see the spectacular wildlife vistas. The state offers a number of wildlife lodges, which are excellent locations for visiting Rajasthan's national parks and wildlife sanctuaries. Vilas Palace in Bharatpur, Ashok Forest Lodge in Bharatpur, Vanya Vilas in Ranthambore, and Ranthambore Bagh in Ranthambore are a few of Rajasthan's well-known wildlife resorts. All of these resorts' guest rooms are tastefully decorated and equipped with contemporary comforts.

SHOPPING

Go on a shopping binge in Rajasthan's crowded bazaars. A huge range of handicrafts, many of which score well on the beauty and quality scale, are overflowing the bazaars. Amazing handicrafts are available to purchase, including fine wooden furniture, exquisitely carved marble sculptures, leather goods, metal products, well-known tie-dye and block-printed textiles, tiny paintings, saris, and jewellery.
